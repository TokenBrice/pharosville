## Question

The May 3 NO-GO on sprite-atlas / OffscreenCanvas-worker / WebGL predates the
201-ship fleet and the 140ms dense CI medians. With V1.1 per-pass data now
available: **can Canvas 2D + the V4.1 program reach 60fps at dense zoom, or
is the ceiling structural?**

## Evidence (V1.1 instrumentation, 2026-06-11, dense fixture, 1440×960)

Local dev machine, 50 samples per view, normal motion, `recovery` scheduler
tier during sampling (Playwright polling inflates RAF pacing, which sheds the
low-priority ambient set — treat ambient as a mild undercount; core passes
are unaffected):

| View | draw median | entity pass | water accents | nameplates | sky | static blit |
|------|------------:|------------:|--------------:|-----------:|----:|------------:|
| Fit zoom 0.81, 96 ships | 4.2–4.8ms | 2.4–2.7ms (~57%) | 1.1–1.2ms | 0 (gated) | 0.1ms | ~0ms |
| Zoom 2.31, 95 ships, 39 plates | 3.4ms | 2.1ms | 0.7ms | 0.3ms | 0.1ms | ~0ms |
| Zoom 2.40, 95 ships, 43 plates | 3.4ms | 1.9ms | 0.7ms | 0.2ms | 0.1ms | ~0ms |

Key findings:

1. **Zoomed-in dense scenes are *cheaper* than fit zoom**, not more expensive
   (fewer water tiles in view; entity pass slightly lighter; nameplates cost
   only ~0.3ms for ~40 plates). The "2–4 fps at 141%/240% zoom" evidence
   captures were automation-load artifacts, as the plan suspected.
2. **Local dense draw is ~4–5ms median against a 16.7ms 60fps budget** —
   ~3.5× headroom on developer hardware with the GPU-composited 2D canvas.
3. The 140ms CI medians are a **4-vCPU CI-environment scaling artifact**
   (~25–30× local), not a product-experience measurement. The CI budget's own
   comment records this (`tests/perf/sustained-motion.spec.ts`).
4. Static-layer caching works as designed: terrain + scene blit is ~0ms per
   frame. The cost centers are the per-frame passes: entity (~60%) and water
   accents (~25%) — both addressable by V4.1 levers (far-zoom fleet LOD,
   accent cadence, draw-call audit) without substrate change.

## Answer

**Canvas 2D is not structurally limited at the current fleet size.** 60fps at
dense zoom is already met locally with 3.5× headroom, and the V4.1 program
targets the two dominant passes directly. No measurement implicates GPU 2D
compositing or main-thread scheduling as a wall. The original NO-GO rationale
(bottleneck is not where a worker/atlas would help; livery recolor can't be a
2D filter) still holds at 201 ships.

The structural risks worth watching are cardinality-driven, not draw-driven:
V3.1 titan poses and V3.5 weathering multiply ship-body-cache keys (V4.2
tracks this), and fleet growth beyond ~400–500 ships would double the entity
pass — still inside budget locally, but it would erode CI margins first.

## Options for the operator

- **Option A — keep the NO-GO (recommended).** Run the V4.1 program against
  the V1.1 numbers; tighten CI budgets to median ≤ 100ms / p95 ≤ 140ms when
  proven. Revisit only if (a) the fleet roadmap exceeds ~2× current ship
  count, or (b) V4.1 lands and dense-zoom medians still exceed budget on
  mid-tier real hardware (not CI).
- **Option B — fund a WebGL/atlas spike anyway.** Only defensible as a bet on
  large future fleet growth or per-pixel effects (livery color matrices,
  bloom) that 2D cannot express. Cost: multi-session migration touching every
  layer, full visual-baseline rebake, new failure modes (context loss,
  shader maintenance). Not supported by current data.

## Decision

- [ ] Operator: keep NO-GO (Option A) / revisit (Option B)

Re-measure triggers for whoever reads this later: fleet > 400 ships, V4.1
complete without reaching CI target, or a feature requiring per-pixel shader
work.
